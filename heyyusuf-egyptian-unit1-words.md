## Notes

- **Gulf ↔ Egyptian consistency**: Each lesson covers the same concepts as its Gulf counterpart. Verify the English translations match 1:1 so the learning progression is identical across dialects.
- **Gender forms**: Egyptian distinguishes male/female in greetings (إزيك vs إزيك with different pronunciation) and questions (اسمك إيه with -ak vs -ik). Both forms are included where relevant.
- **Transliteration style**: Matches the existing Gulf transliteration conventions (capitalized first letter, apostrophe for ع and ء).
- **Cultural notes**: نورتنا (nawwartina) is uniquely Egyptian — it literally means "you lit up our place" and is said as a warm welcome/farewell. تشرفنا (tishrafna) is the Egyptian "nice to meet you."
